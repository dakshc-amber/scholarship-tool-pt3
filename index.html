Selected Work

The Campaigns.

Six OTT pre-release campaigns. One job each time: make the audience want to watch before they can.

01 Disney+ Hotstar

Aapna Bhidu!

Hotstar needed to build anticipation for an upcoming Marvel title with a Hindi-speaking audience — before a single frame was available to the public.

The move: Took Bhidu's viral video format — already living rent-free in the internet's memory — and restructured it as a snarky Marvel recap session. Existing meme language doing the selling.
02 Disney+ Hotstar

Who's the Coolest?

Witty, quirky content to spotlight the film's two lead stars — who play family across two generations on screen — and build buzz pre-release.

The move: Built the entire format around the generational gap itself. A head-to-head on whose era wins. The cast's natural chemistry became the marketing.
03 Amazon Prime Video

The Compatibility Test

TVF Flames was moving to Prime Video for the first time. Brief: tell a teen audience their show had a new home — fast, on a lean production budget.

The move: Simple piece-to-camera scenarios built for speed without losing the show's flirty chemistry. Proof that the smartest idea is often the most produceable one.
04 Netflix

Fan-tastic Comments

Jersey was dropping on Netflix with Shahid Kapoor and Mrunal Thakur. Build anticipation using both leads — and keep it feeling native, not like a promo.

The move: Let real fan comments carry the copy. The audience's own affection for the leads became the campaign, live on YouTube pre-release.
05 Amazon Prime Video

Madhuri's Entry

Madhuri Dixit's first-ever OTT debut headlining a new Prime Video title — a cultural moment that needed copy equal to the weight of it.

The move: Of the wacky, parody, and earnest routes pitched, the chosen execution was the simplest. Knowing when not to be clever is its own creative skill.
06 Amazon Prime Video

Ishita Ki Kahani

A character-led companion piece to the Flames launch — giving the lead her own pre-release voice ahead of the season drop.

The move: Let the character speak directly to camera in her own register. Built a parasocial hook before the audience had pressed play on episode one.
